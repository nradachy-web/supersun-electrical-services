Surge Protection FAQ

Don't I already have surge protection from power strips?

Power strips provide minimal, point-of-use protection — and many don't actually contain surge protection at all (just a power switch). Whole-home surge protection at the panel intercepts surges before they reach any outlet, protecting everything in your home including hardwired appliances that power strips can't cover.

How long does surge protection last?

Quality whole-home surge protectors last for many years. They do degrade over time as they absorb surges (that's their job), so periodic inspection is recommended. We use professional-grade units with indicator lights that tell you when they need replacement.

Will surge protection prevent all damage from a direct lightning strike?

No surge protector can guarantee 100% protection from a direct lightning strike — the energy is simply too massive. However, whole-home surge protection dramatically reduces damage from nearby strikes, grid surges, and the far more common everyday power fluctuations that silently degrade electronics over time.

Can surge protection be added to my existing panel?

Yes. Whole-home surge protection can be added to virtually any existing electrical panel. Installation typically takes about an hour. We'll assess your panel during the consultation and recommend the best solution for your setup.
