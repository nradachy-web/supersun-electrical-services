EV Charger FAQ

What's the difference between Level 1 and Level 2 charging?

Level 1 uses a standard 120V outlet and adds 3-5 miles of range per hour — painfully slow. Level 2 uses a 240V dedicated circuit and adds 25-40 miles per hour, giving you a full charge overnight. We install Level 2 chargers exclusively.

Can my current electrical panel handle an EV charger?

It depends on your panel's capacity and current load. Most modern 200A panels can support an EV charger. Older 100A panels often need upgrading. That's exactly what our consultation evaluates — so you know before any work begins.

Do you install Tesla Wall Connectors?

Yes — we're Tesla Certified installers. We install Tesla Wall Connectors, NEMA 14-50 outlets for Tesla Mobile Connectors, and universal J1772 chargers for all EV brands.

How long does installation take?

Most standard residential installations are completed in 2-4 hours. If a panel upgrade or long wiring run is needed, it may take a full day. We'll give you an accurate timeline during your consultation.
