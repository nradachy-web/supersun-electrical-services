100A to 200A Panel Upgrade

Most homes built before 2000 have 100A panels that can't handle modern loads — EV chargers, smart panels, multiple A/C units. We upgrade to 200A with modern breakers and clean wiring.

Subpanel Installation

Need more circuits but your main panel is full? A subpanel extends your capacity without replacing the main panel. Perfect for garages, workshops, home additions, and pool equipment.

Breaker Replacement

Old, worn, or recalled breakers are a fire hazard. We replace individual breakers or upgrade your entire breaker lineup to modern, code-compliant units — including AFCI and GFCI protection where required.

Code Compliance Updates

Selling your home? Insurance audit? We bring your panel up to current NEC code — proper grounding, bonding, labeling, and arc-fault protection. Pass inspections with confidence.

Federal Pacific & Zinsco Replacement

If your home has a Federal Pacific (Stab-Lok) or Zinsco panel, replacement isn't optional — it's urgent. These panels have documented failure rates and are a known fire risk. We replace them safely and efficiently.

Signs Your Panel Needs Upgrading

Breakers trip frequently. Lights dim when appliances run. Your panel uses fuses instead of breakers. You have a Federal Pacific or Zinsco panel. You're adding an EV charger, hot tub, or major appliance. If any of these apply, schedule a panel assessment today.

Panel Upgrade FAQ

How do I know if I need a panel upgrade?

Common signs include: breakers tripping frequently, lights dimming when appliances run, a fuse box instead of breakers, a panel under 200A, a Federal Pacific or Zinsco panel, or plans to add an EV charger, hot tub, or other high-draw appliance. Our consultation will give you a clear answer.

How long does a panel upgrade take?

Most residential panel upgrades (100A to 200A) are completed in one day — typically 6-8 hours. Your power will be off during part of the upgrade, but we coordinate with your utility to minimize downtime and restore power as quickly as possible.

Will a panel upgrade increase my home's value?

Yes. A modern 200A panel is a selling point for buyers, especially those with EVs or plans for home additions. It also resolves insurance issues — some insurers won't cover homes with outdated panels. It's one of the best electrical investments you can make.

Do I need a permit for a panel upgrade?

Yes, panel upgrades require a permit and inspection in Texas. We handle all permitting and coordinate the city inspection as part of our service. Everything is done by the book — no shortcuts.
